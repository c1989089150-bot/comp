## Iteration 2 Hypotheses

All hypotheses were validated through targeted student interviews and prototype testing, with each hypothesis paired with a corresponding testing and learning cycle (Testing Card/Learning Card). Conclusions are categorised as Refuted, Partially Refuted, or Accepted, with all evidence sourced from the team’s core research documents.

1. Hypothesis: All students face enrolment difficulties due to a disorganised process; international students struggle to verify course prerequisites and degree-aligned course eligibility. Conclusion: Refuted (for all students) / Accepted (for international students). Sources: GuinnessLearningCards.md (Learn Card 1), GuinnessTestingCards.md (Test Card 1).

2. Hypothesis: A customised enrolment application for international students will reduce their course selection errors and streamline the process. Conclusion: Accepted. Sources: GuinnessLearningCards.md (Learn Card 1), GuinnessTestingCards.md (Test Card 2).

3. Hypothesis: Students’ primary course selection issue is uncertainty about which courses to enrol in. Conclusion: Partially Refuted (core issues also include enrolment order, prerequisite compliance, and semester course availability). Sources: GuinnessLearningCards.md (Learn Card 2), XinyueLearningCard.md, XinyueTestingCard.md.

4. Hypothesis: Unclear course sequencing and prerequisites cause significant confusion for students during degree planning. Conclusion: Accepted. Sources: MengfanLearngCards.md (Learn Card 1), MengfanTestingCards.md (Test Card 1).

5. Hypothesis: Students lack clear guidance on how courses align with career paths, leading to uncertainty in course selection. Conclusion: Accepted. Sources: MengfanLearngCards.md (Learn Card 2), MengfanTestingCards.md (Test Card 2).

6. Hypothesis: Lack of transparent course workload, difficulty and assessment information creates stress for students in course planning. Conclusion: Accepted. Sources: MengfanLearngCards.md (Learn Card 3), MengfanTestingCards.md (Test Card 3).

7. Hypothesis: Platform alerts for prerequisites, course sequencing and semester-specific course availability will make course selection easier for students. Conclusion: Accepted. Sources: XinyueLearningCard.md, XinyueTestingCard.md.

8. Hypothesis: Providing more detailed assessment information, including assessment methods, review opportunities, task expectations, and difficulty levels, will make course selection easier for students.
Conclusion: Accepted. Interviews and prototype feedback confirmed that students view the assessment section as one of the most important parts of course information, and that a more detailed and intuitive display significantly improves their ability to evaluate courses.
Sources: ZihanLearningCard.md, ZihanTestingCard.md.
