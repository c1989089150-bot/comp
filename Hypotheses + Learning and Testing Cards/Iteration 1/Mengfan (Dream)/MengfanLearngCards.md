# Learn Card 1
## Hypothesis: 
We believe that unclear course order and prerequisites cause confusion.
## Observation: 
We observed students consistently mentioned difficulty understanding which courses to take first and how prerequisites fit into their degree plan (Interviews 3 & 4).
## Conclusion: 
From that we learn that students need a clear visualization of course sequences and prerequisites.
## Next Step: 
Therefore, we will design a course selection tool that visually maps course order, prerequisites, and recommended paths.

# Learn Card 2
## Hypothesis: 
We believe that students need clearer guidance on how courses relate to career paths.
## Observation: 
We observed many students rely on friends, online reviews, or personal interest, indicating uncertainty about course–career alignment (Interviews 1, 2, 4).
## Conclusion:
From that we learn that students would benefit from career-path mapping for each course.
## Next Step: 
Therefore, we will include a feature in the course selection tool showing course relevance to majors and career paths.

# Learn Card 3
## Hypothesis: 
We believe that lack of workload/difficulty info makes course planning stressful.
## Observation: 
We observed students repeatedly mentioned not knowing difficulty or workload in advance (Interviews 1, 2).
## Conclusion: 
From that we learn that students need transparent info on course workload and difficulty.
## Next Step: 
Therefore, we will add workload/difficulty indicators and assessment type summaries in the course selection tool.
