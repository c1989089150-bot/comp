# Original Opening Questions:
Closed Question: Have you ever enrolled in a subject that you later found out did not count toward your degree requirements? E.g. wasn't in your course guide, was not a valid elective, did not meet prerequisites
Open Question: Can you walk me through how you find out what classes you need to enrol in, and what parts of the enrolment process you find confusing or difficult?


# Refined Question Set:
1. What sources of information or support do you usually use when making course-selection decisions?
2. What information do you wish had been clearer or easier to find when you were choosing your courses?
3. What part of choosing courses feels most risky to get wrong for you? (For examples: finding out what courses you need to enrol in, actually enrolling in them through SI-net, scheduling them on your timetable)
4. Have you ever been unsure whether a course would help you achieve your future career goals? If so, how did you decide whether to take it or not?
5. Have you ever regretted choosing a course because it was too difficult, irrelevant to your major or desired career, or just not useful? What happened?
6. Have you ever had to contact Student Central or another university administrative service to clarify or resolve an issue related to course selection, or courses in general? If so, what was the issue?
7. If there were a tool to help you with course selection, what would you want it to do? (E.g. explain what the course is, how it is relevant to your degree, what career paths it helps with, etc.)


# Refined Question Set (2):
1. Can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
2. Can you walk me through how you usually check prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
3. Imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details — how would you expect to use something like that?
4. What part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you, and what other features would you want it to include?
5. What might stop you from using a tool like this?
6. What would make you trust a website or tool to help with course selection?
7. What would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information resources currently available to you?

Notes: This question set has been designed to gauge more specifically whether our potential solution is something that customers connect with - still using open-ended questions to see what potential features they may want.
