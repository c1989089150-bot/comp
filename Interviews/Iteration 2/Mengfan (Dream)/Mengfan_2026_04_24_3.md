Interview 3:
Age: 19, Degree: Enginnering, International student, Year 2

Speaker 1:
Can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2:
Umm, I’d say I feel kind of supported, but not fully. Like, the information is there, but sometimes it’s a bit confusing.

Speaker 1:
When you say it’s confusing, what exactly makes it confusing?
Speaker 2:
I’m not sure which course I should take first, especially the prerequisites.

Speaker 1:
Can you walk me through how you usually check prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2:
I usually go on the university website and look at the course page. Sometimes I also ask friends if I’m not sure.

Speaker 1:
What kind of information do your friends give that the website doesn’t?
Speaker 2:
They can tell me whether the course is hard or not. Sometimes the website doesn’t explain things clearly.

Speaker 1:
Imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details — how would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2:
I think I’d use it to quickly check prerequisites. Like, just pick a course and see everything straight away.

Speaker 1:
At what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you?
Speaker 2:
Probably when I’m planning my next semester, especially when I’m comparing different courses.

Speaker 1:
What part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you, and what other features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2:
I think the most useful part would be seeing prerequisites clearly. Maybe like a simple diagram or something. And reviews or workload information would also be helpful.

Speaker 1:
Can you explain more about the diagram? More details.
Speaker 2:
Maybe like a flowchart showing which course leads to another. Actually, I don’t have a very clear idea, just something visual.

Speaker 1:
What might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2:
If the information isn’t accurate, I probably wouldn’t use it. Or if it’s too complicated.

Speaker 1:
What would make you trust a website or tool to help with course selection?
Speaker 2:
I’d trust it more if it’s from the university or updated regularly.

Speaker 1:
What would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information resources currently available to you?
Speaker 2:
I think just being simpler and faster would help a lot. Right now everything feels a bit scattered.

Speaker 1:
How many tabs or sources do you normally use?
Speaker 2:
Sometimes like 3 or 4 tabs. It takes time to switch between them.
