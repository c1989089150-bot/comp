Interview 5:
Age: 19, Degree: Media, International student, Year 1

Speaker 1:
How supported do you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2:
I feel somewhat supported, but the information is scattered and not very clear. I often need to rely on others.

Speaker 1:
Can you give an example of when you felt the information was not clear?
Speaker 2:
For example, sometimes I can't tell if I meet the prerequisites or not because the information is spread across different pages.

Speaker 1:
Can you walk me through how you usually check prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2:
I check the course website first, then ask friends or search online if needed.

Speaker 1:
What kind of information do your friends usually give you that the website doesn't?
Speaker 2:
They usually tell me how hard the course is and what the workload is actually like, which the website doesn't explain.

Speaker 1:
Imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details — how would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2:
I would use it to quickly check if I can take a course and compare different options.

Speaker 1:
At what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful?
Speaker 2:
It would be most useful at the early planning stage.

Speaker 1:
What part of a tool like this would be most valuable, and what other features would you want?
Speaker 2:
Clear prerequisite information is the most valuable. I'd also want comparisons and reviews.

Speaker 1:
What might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2:
If the information is inaccurate or the tool is hard to use.

Speaker 1:
What would make you trust a tool like this?
Speaker 2:
I would trust it if the information comes from official or verified sources.

Speaker 1:
What makes you feel a source is “official or verified”?
Speaker 2:
If it comes from the university website or is linked to official course data, I would trust it more.

Speaker 1:
What would make this tool more useful than existing resources?
Speaker 2:
If it combines all information in one place and is easy to understand.
