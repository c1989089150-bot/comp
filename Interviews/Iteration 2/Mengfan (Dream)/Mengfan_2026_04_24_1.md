Interview 1:
Age: 20, Degree: Business, International student, Year 2

Speaker 1:
Can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2:
I think it’s kind of mixed. Like, the university does provide some information online, but sometimes it’s a bit confusing.
I mostly rely on myself or ask friends who’ve done the course before, so I wouldn’t say I feel fully supported.
Honestly, I feel like I have to figure things out by myself most of the time.

Speaker 1:
Can you give a specific example of when the information felt confusing?
Speaker 2:
Yeah, sometimes the prerequisites are written in a complicated way, like using course codes or conditions that are hard to understand.
Also, different pages sometimes show slightly different information, which makes it confusing.

Speaker 1:
Can you walk me through how you usually check prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2:
Usually, I go to the course profile first to check the prerequisites.
Then I might look at the course outline to see the assessment and content.
Sometimes I also search online or ask seniors if I’m not sure.

Speaker 1:
Have you ever missed or misunderstood a prerequisite?
Speaker 2:
Yeah, once I thought I could take a course, but later realised I didn’t meet one of the conditions.
So I had to change my plan.

Speaker 1:
Imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details — how would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2:
I think I would use it a lot, especially when I’m planning my semester.
Instead of checking different pages, I can just click one course and immediately see everything.
It would save me a lot of time.

Speaker 1:
At what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you?
Speaker 2:
Probably at the beginning, when I’m trying to plan my courses for the semester.
That’s when I need to compare different options and make sure I meet all the requirements.

Speaker 1:
Do you compare multiple courses at the same time? How?
Speaker 2:
Yeah, I usually open multiple tabs and switch between them, which can get messy.

Speaker 1:
What part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you, and what other features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2:
The most valuable part would be clearly showing prerequisites.
I’d also like to see things like course difficulty, student reviews, or maybe recommended study plans.
That would help me make better decisions.

Speaker 1:
What might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2:
If the information is not accurate or not updated, I probably wouldn’t trust it.
Also, if it’s too complicated to use, I might just go back to the official website.

Speaker 1:
What would make you trust a website or tool to help with course selection?
Speaker 2:
I would trust it more if the information comes from official university data.
Also, if it’s updated regularly and maybe used by other students, I’d feel more confident using it.

Speaker 1:
Does it matter if other students use it?
Speaker 2:
Yeah, if a lot of students are using it, I’d feel more confident about it.

Speaker 1:
What would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information resources currently available to you?
Speaker 2:
Right now, the information is kind of spread out across different pages.
A tool like this would bring everything together in one place, which is much more convenient.
So it would definitely make the process easier and faster.

Speaker 1:
Which websites do you use most?
Speaker 2:
I usually use the university website the most.
It’s slow and easy to lose track of information.
Sometimes I forget which page had what.
