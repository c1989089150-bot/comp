Interview 2:
Age: 20, Degree: Computer Science, International student, Year 2

Speaker 1:
Can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2:
Um, I’d say I do feel supported. Back in high school, my teachers provided us with a support team that could solve most of our problems. They had some understanding of the Australian university system and first-year foundation courses. But when it comes to specific course details, I still don’t really know much. I kind of have to wait until I actually start studying to find out.

Speaker 1:
What kind of course details do you feel are missing?
Speaker 2:
I don’t know the structure or content before I start.

Speaker 1:
Can you walk me through how you usually check prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2:
I usually use the university website, and also Xiaohongshu. And sometimes I ask my academic advisor or teachers.

Speaker 1:
What kind of information do you usually look for on Xiaohongshu?
Speaker 2:
Mostly student experiences, like whether the course is hard or useful. Sometimes also tips on how to pass.

Speaker 1:
Imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details — how would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2:
Honestly, I think the university website already does a pretty good job with prerequisites. For me, it’s already quite clear, so I haven’t really thought about needing something like that.

Speaker 1:
At what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you?
Speaker 2:
If the website was upgraded and had a more visual tool, I think it would be most useful after I enrol. I usually follow my study plan when enrolling, but after that, I like to check what the course is about. And when I see the prerequisites, I can immediately understand how the course is connected to what I’ve already learned, since those are things I’m already familiar with.

Speaker 1:
What part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you, and what other features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2:
To be honest, I haven’t really thought about it.

Speaker 1:
What might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2:
If the information isn’t accurate, I definitely wouldn’t use it.

Speaker 1:
What would make you trust a website or tool to help with course selection?
Speaker 2:
If I see a lot of positive feedback from students like me on third-party platforms, especially Xiaohongshu, then I’d be willing to try it.

Speaker 1:
What would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information resources currently available to you?
Speaker 2:
Maybe something more visual, like a mind map. It would make course selection more intuitive and help me clearly see the order of study and how different courses are connected.
