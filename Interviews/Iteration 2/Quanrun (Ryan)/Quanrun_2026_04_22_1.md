Interview 1:
Age: 21, Degree: Media, International student, Year 2

Speaker 1: So, can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2: Yes, I often feel overwhelmed by complex course information, and need more initiative support to make confident decisions.
Speaker 1: So, which specific information, like the workload or professor style, is the hardest to find, but the most important for your confidence?
Speaker 2: The most critical but hidden information is the assignment workload, and students' feedback on the professor's teaching style, which help me make decisions effectively.
Speaker 1: So, can you talk about how you usually check course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2: Yes, I usually have to manually search through PDF handbooks or click through multiple individual pages in the school portal.
Speaker 1: And imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details. How would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2: I would use its interactive map to visualize course connections and maybe apply a mock schedule feature.
Speaker 1: OK, so at what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you?
Speaker 2: It would be most useful during the pre-enrollment period before official registration opens.
Speaker 1: Could this tool also be useful during the add or drop week when you need to make quick last-minute minor changes?
Speaker 2: Yes, it would be extremely valuable during the add or drop week for quickly surveying the requirements of alternative courses when my first choices are full.
Speaker 1: OK, so what part of a tool like this would be the most valuable to you? And what other future features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2: The visual course map is the most valuable, but I would also want features like credit tracking and difficulty ratings.
Speaker 1: OK, the next question is: what might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2: I would stop using the tool if the information becomes inaccurate or lags behind official university updates.
Speaker 1: OK, so what would make you trust a tool to help you with course selections?
Speaker 2: I would trust the tool if it uses verified official data and has positive reviews from other students.
Speaker 1: OK, the last question: What would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information resources currently available to you?
Speaker 2: It is more useful because it’s interactive and user-friendly. OK, thank you very much.
