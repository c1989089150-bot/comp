Interview 2:
Age: 21, Degree: Business, International student, Year 2

Speaker 1: The first question: Can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2: Nowhere close. Not very supported. The resources are spread across different websites, so I often feel lost. And I usually end up asking friends for help, which isn't always reliable.
Speaker 1: Can you tell me about how you usually check the prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2: Well, I go to the course explorer, download the PDF, and search for the prerequisites. The wording is sometimes confusing, and it takes me a long time to check everything.
Speaker 1: OK, so if there’s a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details, how would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2: I would expect it to be simple. I click a course and see a clear list of prerequisites, workload, and assignment deadlines. Ideally, I want all important information in one place without opening multiple pages.
Speaker 1: So, at what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you?
Speaker 2: At the very beginning, when I am still deciding which courses to take, and also near the enrollment deadline to double-check that I haven't missed any requirements.
Speaker 1: Next question: What part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you? And what other features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2: Well, the prerequisite checker would be the most valuable. I would also like difficulty ratings from past students, assignment breakdowns, and a warning if a course has a heavy workload.
Speaker 1: What might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2: If the tool is not updated regularly, I wouldn't trust it. Also, if the interface is too complicated or slow, I would go back to the official university pages.
Speaker 1: What would make you trust a website or tool to help with course selection?
Speaker 2: Well, if it clearly shows that the information comes from official sources like university course profiles. Also, if it is recommended by the university or by other students I know.
Speaker 1: Last question: What would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information resources currently available to you?
Speaker 2: Current sources are scattered. If this tool combines timetables, assignment details, and student reviews in one simple view, that would be much better for me. OK, thanks so much. Goodbye.
