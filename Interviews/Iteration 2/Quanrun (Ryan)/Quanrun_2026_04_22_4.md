Interview 2:
Age: 22, Degree: Computer Science, International student, Year 3

Speaker 1: So, can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2: To be honest, I feel only moderately supported when making course selection decisions. The information is available, but it is often scattered and not very intuitive. Sometimes, I really rely more on advice from other students or my friends.
Speaker 1: So, I’d like to know how you usually check the course information before enrolling.
Speaker 2: Well, I usually check course profiles on the UQ website, and sometimes I will open multiple tabs to compare courses. After that, maybe sometimes, if it is very complicated, I might double-check with my program coordinator or just confirm later.
Speaker 1: OK, so if there’s a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its information and key details, how would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2: Well, if there was a tool that shows the prerequisites instantly, I think I would use it to quickly check and efficiently plan future semesters.
Speaker 1: OK, so at what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you?
Speaker 2: I think the most useful time is before the enrollment period.
Speaker 1: Yeah, so what part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you, and what other features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2: I think it’s maybe the student reviews and timetable conflict checking.
Speaker 1: Yeah, yeah. And what might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2: Well, I think if the website or the app is very complicated, I won’t use it.
Speaker 1: And what would make you choose a website or a tool to help you with course selection?
Speaker 2: I would trust the tool more if the data comes from official UQ sources.
Speaker 1: Yeah, and what would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information sources currently available to you?
Speaker 2: I think this tool can help me know more about the course, and I think it will save lots of time.
Speaker 1: OK, that's all. Thank you very much.
