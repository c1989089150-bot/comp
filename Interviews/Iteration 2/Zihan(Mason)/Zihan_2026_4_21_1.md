Speaker 1: Can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2: I think I did not get much support from the school because the school does not provide much information directly to students. Most of my support comes from my friends and social media.
Speaker 1: Can you walk me through how you usually check prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2: Usually, I copy the course code and search it in Safari. Then I check the course profile to see details about the assessments and what the course content is about.
Speaker 1: Imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details. How would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2: I imagine it as an app. In the app, all the courses would already be listed, and students could choose a course and see the course content, prerequisites, and assessment information directly.
Speaker 1: At what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you, and why?
Speaker 2: I think it would be most useful when I first enter school, like as a freshman. At that time, I do not have much information about the courses, so I can use the app to quickly understand what I am going to study in the future.
Speaker 1: What part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you, and what other features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2: The most valuable part is that it can save a lot of time. I do not need to copy the course code into the browser every time. I can directly check what the course is about, what I need to do in the semester, and the assessment information.
Speaker 1: What might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2: If it charges money, I probably would not use it anymore.
Speaker 1: What would make you trust a website or tool to help with course selection?
Speaker 2: I would trust it more if it was an official website or built by the school. If the school creates the tool, I think it would be more reliable than other websites.
Speaker 1: What would make a tool like this more useful than other course information sources currently available to you?
Speaker 2: It is similar to what I said before. It saves a lot of time for me. I can clearly see what I need to do in the semester, what the course content is, and what the assessments are. In this way, I do not need to waste time searching everywhere.
