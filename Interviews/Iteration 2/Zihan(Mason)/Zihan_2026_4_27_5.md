Speaker 1: Can you tell me about how supported you feel when making course selection decisions?
Speaker 2: I would say not very supported, especially at the beginning as a new student. It is quite hard to understand all the course information and catch the key points when making course selection decisions. Sometimes there is too much information, but not enough clear guidance, so it can feel confusing.
Speaker 1: Can you walk me through how you usually check prerequisites or course information before enrolling?
Speaker 2: I usually start with the course handbook or the official course page. I also check the related course information. Then, if I am still not sure, I will ask classmates or staff.
Speaker 1: Imagine a tool where you could select a course and immediately see its prerequisites and key details — how would you expect to use something like that?
Speaker 2: I would use it during course selection to quickly understand what a course is about, what I need before taking it, and what kind of jobs it might be related to in the future. I think that would make course selection much easier because I could choose courses based on both academic requirements and future career plans.
Speaker 1: At what point in your course selection process would a tool like this be most useful to you?
Speaker 2: As I said earlier, it would be helpful before enrolling in courses. I could use it when I am comparing different course options and trying to decide which ones are suitable for me.
Speaker 1: What part of a tool like this would be most valuable to you, and what other features would you want it to include?
Speaker 2: Actually, the best part would be the convenience. Having everything in one place would make it much easier to use. I would also want to see comments from previous students because that would give me a better idea of what the course is actually like.
Speaker 1: What might stop you from using a tool like this?
Speaker 2: If the information is wrong or outdated, I would not trust it or use it.
Speaker 1: What would make you trust a website or tool to help with course selection?
Speaker 2: If there is some quantitative data or official information to support it, I would trust it more. For example, if the information comes from the university or is clearly updated regularly, that would make it more reliable.
Speaker 1: What would make a tool like this more useful than the other course information resources currently available to you?
Speaker 2: It would be more useful if everything was in one place. For example, if I want to check the course profile, prerequisites, or related information, I would not need to open many different pages like the course page or library resources. It would redirect me clearly and save a lot of time, so it would be much easier than before.
