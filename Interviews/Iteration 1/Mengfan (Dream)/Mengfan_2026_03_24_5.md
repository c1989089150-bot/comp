[Speaker 1]
what sources of information or support do you usually use when making course-selection decisions?
[Speaker 2]
Um, I mostly get information from the university, like the website and student support. And also just the online information that’s available there.
[Speaker 1]
Okay, good. What information do you wish had been clearer or easier to find when you were choosing your courses?
[Speaker 2]
I think information about the subjects — like what they’re about and how useful they are — is pretty easy to find.
But maybe more details about fees and how everything is structured would be helpful.
[Speaker 1]
Right. And what part of choosing courses feels most risky to get wrong for you? Like finding the right courses, enrolling, or scheduling them?
[Speaker 2]
I feel like it’s choosing the correct courses, especially at the beginning of the semester.
I guess that’s probably the most difficult part.
[Speaker 1]
Yeah, that makes sense. Have you ever been unsure whether a course would help you achieve your future career goals? If so, how did you decide whether to take it or not?
[Speaker 2]
I think it mostly depends on my interests, because if you’re not interested, there’s no point doing something you won’t like in the future.
But sometimes, even if you enjoy something, it might not always be the best for your career. So yeah, it can be a bit hard to decide.
[Speaker 1]
Okay. Have you ever regretted choosing a course because it was too difficult, irrelevant, or just not useful? What happened?
[Speaker 2]
No, not really. I’ve chosen what I like, and I’m pretty happy with it so far.
But some courses can be difficult, and sometimes you realise later that a subject might not be exactly what you expected.
[Speaker 1]
Yeah, fair enough. Have you ever had to contact Student Central or another university service to clarify or resolve an issue related to course selection?
[Speaker 2]
No, I haven’t had to contact them yet.
[Speaker 1]
if there were a tool to help you with course selection, what would you want it to do?
[Speaker 2]
Maybe something that lists courses with feedback from other students.
Like, a place where people can share their experiences, what they liked or didn’t like, and any advice.
So other students don’t make the same mistakes and know what to expect before choosing.
