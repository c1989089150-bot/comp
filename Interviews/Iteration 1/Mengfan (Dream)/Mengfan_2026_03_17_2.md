Interview 2:
Age: 19, Degree: Media, International student, Year 1

[Speaker 1]
What sources of information or support do you usually use when making course-selection decisions?
[Speaker 2]
I would like to say I usually rely on the university website, like, I always use course descriptions, my.UQ, and course profiles. I also ask senior students for advice. On the other hand, I sometimes check social media or classmates’ feedback to understand the difficulty level and assessment types of the courses.
[Speaker 1]
And what information do you wish had been clearer or easier to find when you were choosing your courses?
[Speaker 2]
Well, for this question, I wish it could tell me the actual difficulty, like, types of assessments, and workload were clearer. For example, it would be better understood if the course profile told us whether the course focuses more on essays or presentations. This would also be helpful if the connection between courses and future career paths was explained more explicitly.
[Speaker 1]
Good answer. And what part of choosing courses feels most risky to get wrong for you?
[Speaker 2]
I think the most risky part is choosing courses without fully understanding the content. And also, timetable conflicts can be a big problem. You know sometimes a course looks interesting at first, but later I realize it’s quite different from what I expected.
[Speaker 1]
Have you ever been unsure whether a course would help you achieve your future career goals? If so, how did you decide whether to take it or not?
[Speaker 2]
Yes, I have. I usually review the course profile carefully, ask lecturers or senior students, and consider whether the course aligns with my major or future career goals before making a decision.
[Speaker 1]
Perfect. Have you ever regretted choosing a course because it was too difficult, irrelevant to your major or not useful? What happened?
[Speaker 2]
Yes, there were some courses that were either too difficult or not very interesting, and not very relevant to my goals.
I ended up spending a lot of time on them, but I didn’t gain much value from the experience.
[Speaker 1]
Have you ever had to contact Student Central or another university administrative service to clarify or resolve an issue related to course selection?
[Speaker 2]
Yes, I have contacted Student Central regarding enrollment issues and timetable conflicts. They helped me confirm course requirements and also resolve some scheduling problems.
[Speaker 1]
If there were a tool to help you with course selection, what would you want it to do?
[Speaker 2]
I would like a tool that can clearly explain course content and learning outcomes. It should also show the difficulty level and actual workload, and indicate how the course relates to my degree and possible career paths. And it would be very helpful if it could provide student reviews and automatically check timetable conflicts and degree requirements.
