Speaker 1: The first question, could you tell me where you usually get information or help when you are choosing classes?
Speaker 2: English isn't my first language. I start by scrolling through the UK official website to check course online and major rules. But those professional words are so confusing, and there are still no info in my native language Chinese. Most of the time, I just ask my Chinese friends who have taken those classes before.
Speaker 1: I can imagine how tough it is. So, if there were a tool to help you with course selections, what would you want it to do?
Speaker 2: It'd be awesome if it lists what jobs this course is good for, like which industries care about it. And maybe it has reviews from other international students, so I know if it's too hard for non-native English speakers or if the assignments are fair.
Speaker 1: Have you ever regretted picking a class? Like, because it was too hard, or not related to your major or future job, or just not useful?
Speaker 2: 100% yes. I picked a native class called Australian Local Culture, thinking it'd help me fit in more. But after a few weeks, I realized it's all about the Roman, Australian history and customs, and totally nothing to do with my major.
Speaker 1: Let's move on to the next question. What part of choosing courses scares you the most to mess up? Like figuring out which classes to take, ensuring enrollment or timetable clashes, whatever you feel most worried about.
Speaker 2: I'm always worried I will miss a core class, or if a class is withdrawn one day and it'll delay my graduation. And picking classes is also stressful.
Speaker 1: Have you ever been unsure if a class would help your future career? Or how did you decide whether to take it or not?
Speaker 2: All the time. Last semester, I had two electives to pick from—Data Analysis and Computer Graphics. I want to be a software engineer, but I had no idea which one was more useful. We mostly just rely on other Chinese students' experience. It is not perfect, but it is the easiest way for us.
Speaker 1: Last question. When you were picking classes before, is there any information you wish was easier to find or clearer? Like something that confused you a lot.
Speaker 2: Definitely, the course difficulty. The official sites only say what you learn, not how hard it is to learn. It is so hard for us international students.
