Interview 5:Age:19 Degree:UQ college student 
Speaker 1: What sources of information or support do you usually use when making course-selection decisions? 
Speaker 2: I usually look at the UQ course profile. And I’ll ask friends or previous students about what the course is actually like.
Speaker 1: What information do you wish had been clearer or easier to find when you were choosing your courses? 
Speaker 2:I wish the workload were clearer. Sometimes the course description looks short and easy, but as I learned, it turns out to be much heavier than I expected.
Speaker 1: What part of choosing courses feels most risky to get wrong for you? (For examples: finding out what courses you need to enrol in, actually enrolling in them through SI-net, scheduling them on your timetable) 
Speaker 2: I probably chose the wrong course for my degree requirements. I’m always worried I might miss a required course or pick one that doesn’t count the way I thought.
Speaker 1: Have you ever been unsure whether a course would help you achieve your future career goals? If so, how did you decide whether to take it or not? 
Speaker 2: Yep, definitely. I usually check what skills the course teaches and whether those skills match the kind of jobs I’m interested in. If it feels useful, I’ll take it.
Speaker 1: Have you ever regretted choosing a course because it was too difficult, irrelevant to your major or desired career, or just not useful? What happened? 
Speaker 2: Yeah, I have. I took a course that sounded interesting at first, but later I realised it wasn’t really useful for what I want to do. I still finished it, because the period for no-fault withdrawal of classes has already passed.
Speaker 1: Have you ever had to contact Student Central or another university administrative service to clarify or resolve an issue related to course selection, or courses in general? If so, what was the issue? 
Speaker 2: No, I don't have this experience; I always ask the college via email.
Speaker 1: If there were a tool to help you with course selection, what would you want it to do? (E.g. explain what the course is, how it is relevant to your degree, what career paths it helps with, etc.) 
Speaker 2:I’d want it to explain courses in a simple way and include some student reviews, because that would really help me when choosing courses.
Speaker 1: thank you.
