Interview 3:Age:21 Degree:Computer Science International Student year2
Speaker 1: What sources of information or support do you usually use when making course-selection decisions?
Speaker 2: For me, I always check the course profile on the UQ website. I also look at some group chats where students share their experiences about different courses. And it is very easy for me to get to know the course information.
Speaker 1: What information do you wish had been clearer or easier to find when you were choosing your courses?
Speaker 2: I wish the university could provide more information about final exams.I can prepare for the exam better and have a higher chance of passing the final exam.
Speaker 1: What part of choosing courses feels most risky to get wrong for you? (For examples: finding out what courses you need to enrol in, actually enrolling in them through SI-net, scheduling them on your timetable)
Speaker 2: I think choosing the right courses for my degree is the most risky part, especially making sure I meet all the requirements and do not miss anything important.
Speaker 1: Have you ever been unsure whether a course would help you achieve your future career goals? If so, how did you decide whether to take it or not?
Speaker 2: Yes. Usually I would ask my academic advisors or education supervisors, and I would also check what skills the course teaches before deciding whether to take it.
Speaker 1: Have you ever regretted choosing a course because it was too difficult, irrelevant to your major or desired career, or just not useful? What happened?
Speaker 2: Yep. I took CSSE1001 last year, and it was quite difficult for me because it required a lot of programming. I found it hard to keep up, and in the end I did not get a good grade.
Speaker 1: Have you ever had to contact Student Central or another university administrative service to clarify or resolve an issue related to course selection, or courses in general? If so, what was the issue?
Speaker 2: Yes, I contacted Student Central because I was confused about what I needed to enrol in.
Speaker 1: If there were a tool to help you with course selection, what would you want it to do? (E.g. explain what the course is, how it is relevant to your degree, what career paths it helps with, etc.)
Speaker 2: To be honest, I have no idea. Um, I want a tool which can show each course clearly. And show it related to my degree. And suggest that courses based on the student career goals.
