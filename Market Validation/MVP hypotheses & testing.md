# Minimum Viable Product Analysis
## MVP Feature Hypotheses
Based on our research findings, we defined the following MVP hypotheses:

H1: Students need a centralised system integrates course information to reduce time spent searching across multiple platforms.

H2: Students need clear prerequisite checks and warning indicators to determine eligibility and avoid 
mistakes.

H3: Filtering and comparison features will improve course selection efficiency and accuracy.

H4: Students need structured summaries, recommendations, and guidance to reduce uncertainty

H5: International students need additional guidance, simplified explanations and clearer terminology when using the course selection system.

## Learning Cards (Linked Summary)
#### Learning 1: 
Students struggle with scattered course information and spend too much time comparing options.
Students reported needing to open multiple platforms and tabs to gather course information.
This confirms that information fragmentation is a major issue, supporting the need for a centralised system.
#### Learning 2: 
The main issue is not enrollment errors, but uncertainty in decision-making.
Students are often unsure whether they meet prerequisites and tend to double-check with peers or other sources.
This shows that clear prerequisite checks and warning indicators are necessary to reduce uncertainty.
#### Learning 3: 
Students struggle with scattered course information and spend too much time comparing options.
Students find it difficult to efficiently compare courses due to inconsistent and dispersed information.
This highlights the need for structured filtering and comparison features to improve efficiency.
#### Learning 4: 
The main issue is not enrollment errors, but uncertainty in decision-making.
Students rely heavily on friends, personal judgment, and external validation when making course decisions.
This suggests that students need guidance, recommendations, and structured summaries to increase confidence.
#### Learning 5:
International students experience more confusion due to unfamiliar systems. Many international students reported difficulty understanding terminology and navigating the system. This confirms the need for simplified explanations, clearer terminology, and additional guidance.
<br/><br/>

## MVP Direction
Based on testing results, the MVP should focus on:
#### Centralised course information platform
Integrating course details into one system to reduce time spent searching across multiple platforms and improve accessibility.
#### Filtering and comparison tools
Allowing students to efficiently compare courses and select relevant options based on their needs.
#### Decision support (e.g. recommendations or course guidance)
Providing structured summaries, suggestions, and guidance to reduce uncertainty and improve decision confidence.
#### Onboarding/support for international students
Offering simplified explanations, clearer terminology, and step-by-step guidance to support users unfamiliar with the system.
