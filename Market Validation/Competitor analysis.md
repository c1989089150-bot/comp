# Competitor Analysis
| Competitor company | Target market | Key value propositions | Weaknesses | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DegreeWorks | Undergraduate and graduate students across all years, focusing on degree progress tracking | Visual degree progress tracking; alerts for unmet degree requirements; basic course recommendation based on remaining credits; integrates with university registration systems. | Limited elective course filtering (no difficulty/semester preferences); no support for international students; no career relevance analysis; manual course registration required. | Strong in degree progress management and academic auditing, helping students and advisors monitor graduation progress and transfer credits effectively. |
| Stellic | Universities, academic advisors, students | Helps students plan their entire degree; tracking their progress towards graduation, highlighting prerequisites and streamlines course registration | Could be seen as too complex or expensive for an average student: Hidden behind a paywall meaning it would be more effective if universities adopt it first, rather than individual students | A lot broader than our solution - covers multiple areas that we havent researched into. |
| StudyPlanner | Universities, students, academic advisors | Creates personalized study plans, while applying various curriculum rules. Checks prerequisites, sequences courses in accordance, and helps students navigate enrolment pathways. | Requires more university integration to ensure current and accurate course information. Less effective when adopted by individual students. Also significantly more complicated than the scope of our product - might be difficult for the average student to navigate. | Pretty relevent because it places particular focus on meeting prerequisites. This product is also more focused within the Australian market, so could be more relevant. |
| MyTimetable(like UQ) | Australian university students who need to plan class times after enrolment. | Helps students view available class options, plan preferred timetable, and select tutorials/practicals/lectures that fit their schedule. | Mainly focuses on timetable planning after enrolment; does not strongly support course recommendation, prerequisite checking, graduation planning, or career relevance analysis. | Relevant to Australian universities and student enrolment experience, but it solves scheduling more than course selection. |
| CourseLoop | Universities, curriculum teams, academic administrators, and students who access official course information. | Provides curriculum management, course catalogue publishing, and study plan information. It helps universities keep course data accurate, consistent, and easier to publish for students. | More focused on university administration and curriculum management than direct student course-selection support. It may not provide personalised recommendations based on course difficulty, career relevance, international student needs, or individual study preferences. | Useful Australian-relevant competitor because it supports accurate course and curriculum information. However, it is not mainly designed as a student-facing course selection assistant, so our product can focus more on personalised decision-making. |
